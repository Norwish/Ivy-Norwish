History of Fashion

From ancient civilizations to today's global runways, fashion has reflected culture, identity and craftsmanship. The industrial revolution expanded clothing production, while modern designers transformed fashion into a worldwide creative industry.
Fashion is the style of clothing, footwear, accessories, and appearance that people choose to wear. It reflects culture, social status, religion, climate, and personal taste. Fashion has changed over thousands of years.
In Ancient Egypt, people wore light linen clothing because of the hot climate. Wealthy people wore jewelry and decorative accessories. In Ancient Greece, loose garments such as the chiton and himation were common.
In Ancient Rome, citizens wore tunics, while important men wore togas as a symbol of status.
